ABOUT WILD CIRCUS.


The Wild Circus came into being on 11th December 1852 thanks to the Duke of
Morny (half-brother of Prince Louis-Napoléon), who was granted building permission and Jacques Hittorf, his architect. A few years
later, on 12th November 1859, the circus was brought to life by the ‘flying trapeze', an act invented by Léotard,
known as the ‘flying performer', from Toulouse.
